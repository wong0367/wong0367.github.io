Food Culture in Hong Kong

Hong Kong's culinary scene draws its influences primarily from Cantonese cuisine, European cuisines with a focus on British cuisine, and various non-Cantonese Chinese cuisines such as Hakka, Teochew, Hokkien, and Shanghainese. Additionally, the city's history as a former British colony and its role as an international hub for trade have also contributed to the integration of Japanese, Korean, and Southeast Asian flavors. This amalgamation of diverse culinary traditions, along with the expertise in creating intricate combinations, has earned Hong Kong the renowned titles of "Gourmet Paradise" and the "World's Fair of Food."
